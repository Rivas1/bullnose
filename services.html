Preparation is Key

The magic really lies in how well you can prepare. This is the most time consuming part of the process and the most critical step.

Sanding for Smooth Finish

Since we must remove the old paint, sometimes imperfections in the wood become apparent. We use color-matched resin or silicone to fill cracks and sloppy unions. Then, we sand.

Wood Stain

Not only do we do solid colors, we can also apply stain to display the natural grain of the wood.

Spray Finish

Finally, we use a compressed air system to ensure even coats of laquer/sealant. This system allows us to drastically minimize turnaround time.
